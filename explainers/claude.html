What is Claude?

An AI assistant built by Anthropic to be helpful, harmless, and honest. Not just a text predictor ‚Äî a reasoner trained to think carefully and be genuinely useful.

Claude is a large language model (LLM) made by Anthropic. It reads your input, reasons about what a helpful, honest response looks like, and generates it ‚Äî drawing on broad training and careful alignment work.

The Short Version

Claude is trained on vast amounts of text and fine-tuned using human feedback to be helpful, accurate, and safe. It can write, reason, code, analyze, and converse across nearly any topic.

Who Made It

Anthropic ‚Äî an AI safety company founded in 2021. Their focus is building AI that is reliable, interpretable, and aligned with human intentions, not just capable.

What Makes It Different

Claude is trained with a strong emphasis on constitutional AI ‚Äî a set of principles guiding how it reasons about what's helpful vs. harmful. It's designed to push back on bad requests, admit uncertainty, and avoid overconfidence.

Not Lookup ‚Äî Reasoning

Claude doesn't retrieve pre-written answers. It generates a response token by token, reasoning through the problem as it goes. For harder questions, it can think step-by-step in an extended thinking mode before answering.

‚ñº
Context Window
Claude processes everything in a "context window" ‚Äî your messages, previous turns, uploaded files, instructions. It attends to all of this simultaneously rather than linearly. Longer contexts let Claude handle complex tasks without losing track, but very long contexts can reduce focus on specific details.
‚ñº
Chain-of-Thought
For complex problems, Claude reasons step by step ‚Äî breaking the problem into parts, working through each, then synthesizing an answer. This dramatically improves accuracy on math, logic, and multi-step tasks. You can prompt Claude to "think step by step" to make this explicit.
‚ñº
Uncertainty & Calibration
Claude is trained to be calibrated ‚Äî confident when it has good reason, uncertain when it doesn't. It will say "I'm not sure" or "you should verify this" when appropriate. Overconfident AI is more dangerous than cautious AI, so this is intentional.
‚ñº
No Memory Between Sessions
By default, Claude starts fresh each conversation. It has no memory of past sessions unless the application explicitly passes that context in. Within a session, it remembers everything said so far. This is a privacy feature ‚Äî your conversations aren't used to train the model.
‚ñº
Knowledge Cutoff
Claude's training data has a cutoff date ‚Äî it doesn't know about events after that point unless given tools like web search. It can tell you its cutoff date if asked. For current events, Claude can use search tools when available, or acknowledge that its information may be outdated.

Tips for Getting the Best Results

Claude responds well to clear context. The more you tell it about what you need, why, and for whom ‚Äî the better the output.

‚ñº
Be Specific About What You Want
Instead of "write an email", try "write a professional email to a client explaining a project delay, keeping it under 150 words, apologetic but confident." The more constraints you give, the less Claude has to guess ‚Äî and the better the result.
‚ñº
Give Context and Audience
Claude adapts its style dramatically based on audience. "Explain quantum entanglement to a 10-year-old" vs "to a physics PhD" produces completely different responses. Always state who this is for ‚Äî it matters more than most people think.
‚ñº
Ask It to Think Step by Step
For complex reasoning tasks, add "think step by step" or "show your reasoning." This activates more deliberate reasoning and dramatically improves accuracy on math, logic puzzles, multi-step planning, and code debugging.
‚ñº
Iterate, Don't Restart
You don't need a perfect prompt on the first try. Start with a draft, get Claude's output, then refine: "make it shorter", "make the tone less formal", "add a section on X." Treating it as a conversation rather than a one-shot query usually gets better results.
‚ñº
Push Back and Ask Why
Claude's first answer isn't always its best. Ask it to reconsider, challenge its reasoning, or try a different approach. It's designed to be a thinking partner ‚Äî and it will genuinely update when you give it good reasons to.
